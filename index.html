The Essential Workplace Toolkit

Master the polite, clear, and efficient phrases needed for routine office tasks—from asking for technical help and sharing files to arranging meetings and navigating office etiquette.

Series 1: Logistics and Technical Requests — Asking for Help Clearly

Asking for Technical Assistance

  • Can you help me with the printer? What's the issue? Asking for assistance with equipment, followed by asking for **clarification** of the problem.
  • Did it update your computer? Any improvements? Checking on completed IT work and asking for **feedback** on the outcome.
  • Is the projector in the conference room working? Can you help me set it up later? Verifying the status of shared equipment and making a polite, conditional **request for help**.
  • I've lost my phone charger in the office. Have you checked the lost and found? Describing a problem and giving a **practical suggestion** for a solution.

Office Supplies and Environment

  • Do we need to order more office supplies? I'll order some, anything else? Initiating a logistics check and following up by **offering to take action**.
  • Is it just me or is the office too noisy today? Can we ask everyone to keep it down? Raising a discomfort and proposing a **gentle corrective action**.
  • Who is in charge of managing office supplies? Do we contact them for specific requests? Identifying the **responsible department** and clarifying the correct procedure for requests.
  • Is the emergency evacuation drill this week? Do you know what time it will start? Confirming a scheduled event and asking for a **specific detail** (time).
COMMON TERMS
Logistics: The detailed organization and implementation of a complex operation.
Take a Look: A polite offer to quickly examine a problem or request.
Set it Up: To prepare equipment or a space for use.

Series 2: Project Updates & Information Sharing — Confirming Flow and Status

Project Status Checks

  • Are we on track for the project deadline? Great, keep up the good work. Checking progress and immediately offering **positive reinforcement**.
  • How is the project going? What about the budget? Asking for a general update, then narrowing the focus to a **specific metric** (budget).
  • Are you prepared for the interdepartmental meeting? Do you need any help with that? Assessing readiness and **proactively offering assistance** with preparation.
  • Have you started on the annual report? Need any data from me? Checking project kickoff and offering to provide **needed resources**.

Document & Email Flow

  • Did you receive my email? Please let me know if you have any feedback. Confirming receipt and explicitly **soliciting feedback** for the next step.
  • Can you share the project file with me? Let me know if it's too large to email. Requesting a document and providing a **contingency plan** for technical issues.
  • Did you finish the client proposal? Any response yet? Checking completion status and asking about the **external response timeline**.
  • How did my presentation go? Any areas for improvement? Seeking performance feedback and explicitly asking for **constructive criticism**.
COMMON TERMS
Ahead of Schedule: Completing tasks faster than planned.
Contingency Plan: An alternative action for when things go wrong.
Proof Reading: Reviewing text for errors before submission.

Series 3: Planning and Scheduling — Managing Time and Meetings

Meetings and Appointments

  • Can we reschedule our 3:00 p.m. meeting? What time do you suggest? Making a polite request to change a meeting and immediately **proposing alternatives**.
  • Are we still doing the weekly team check-ins? What time is it again? Confirming a recurring event and asking for **time verification**.
  • Do you have the meeting agenda? Great, let's go over it together. Confirming the presence of a key document and inviting **immediate collaboration**.
  • How often are our team meetings? What time are they usually at? Asking for the **frequency** and **time** of a standard recurring meeting.

Social and Travel Planning

  • Where shall we go for lunch today? How about the new Italian place? Initiating a lunch plan and making a **specific recommendation**.
  • Shall we plan a team lunch this Friday? How about that new Mexican restaurant? Proposing a social event for morale and **suggesting a location**.
  • Have you booked your travel arrangements? Don't forget to arrange airport transportation. Checking travel status and giving a **helpful reminder** about logistics.
  • Would you like to take a coffee break? Which coffee do you prefer? Offering a short break and then asking for a **preference**.
COMMON TERMS
Check-In: A quick, regular meeting to review status or progress.
On Track: Progressing as expected toward a goal.
Go Over: To review or discuss something in detail.

Series 4: Office Life & HR/Wellness — Culture, Policy, and Development

HR and Policy

  • Do you understand our health insurance plan? Shall we ask HR for a clarification session? Identifying confusion about a policy and proposing a **group solution** via HR.
  • Are you familiar with the company's social media policy? Let's review it together to ensure compliance. Checking policy knowledge and offering **collaborative review** for compliance.
  • How do I submit travel expenses for reimbursement? Use the online form on the HR portal. Asking for a **procedure** and providing a direct, specific answer.
  • Did you hear about the office relocation? Next month, we're moving downtown. Asking about a major change and providing the **key detail** (timeline/location).

Wellness and Office Culture

  • Can we remind everyone to keep the kitchen clean? I'll send out an email reminder. Identifying a shared problem and suggesting a **courteous public reminder**.
  • Have you joined the health and wellness program? How is it going so far? Asking about participation and **soliciting feedback** on the experience.
  • What are some good stress relief activities? Yoga sessions or team sports. Asking for suggestions and providing **group-focused activity ideas**.
  • It's Lisa's work anniversary tomorrow. We should definitely acknowledge that. Sharing a social detail and proposing a **celebratory action**.
COMMON TERMS
Compliance: Acting in accordance with a rule or policy.
Clarification Session: A meeting dedicated to explaining confusing information.
Work Anniversary: The yearly date an employee started at the company.
